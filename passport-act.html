Passport Act 2020 (PL 2020-18)

Republic of the Marshall Islands

Section 301: Entitlement to Passport
Every citizen of the Republic of the Marshall Islands shall be entitled to a passport, subject to the provisions of this Act and regulations made under it.

Section 302: Application for Passport
An application for a passport shall be made in the prescribed form and manner and shall be accompanied by such documents and information as may be required.

Section 303: Issuing Authority
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade is the designated authority responsible for issuing passports in accordance with this Act.

Section 304: Types of Passports
The Minister may by regulation provide for different types of passports including diplomatic, official, and ordinary passports.

Section 305: Validity and Renewal
A passport issued under this Act shall be valid for a period as prescribed by regulation and may be renewed subject to the requirements set forth by the issuing authority.

Section 306: Refusal to Issue or Cancellation
The issuing authority may refuse to issue or may cancel a passport if the applicant is not eligible or if the passport was obtained through fraud or misrepresentation.

Section 307: Surrender of Passport
A person shall surrender their passport upon the request of the issuing authority under circumstances defined by regulation or national security interest.

Section 308: Offenses and Penalties
Any person who forges, alters, or uses a passport in contravention of this Act commits an offense and is liable on conviction to penalties prescribed by regulation.

Section 309: Ownership of Passports
All passports remain the property of the Republic of the Marshall Islands and shall be returned upon demand by the issuing authority.

Section 310: False Statements
Any applicant who knowingly makes a false statement or provides false documents in connection with a passport application commits an offense punishable by law.

Section 311: Regulations
The Minister may make regulations for the purpose of giving effect to the provisions of this Act, including application procedures, fees, formats, and other administrative details.

Section 312: Repeal
This Act repeals any previous passport-related legislation inconsistent with the provisions stated herein.
